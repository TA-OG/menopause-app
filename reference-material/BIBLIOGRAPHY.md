## NIGERIAN / WEST AFRICAN DIETARY REFERENCES

### BusinessDay NG 2021 — NIGERIAN TRADITIONAL DIETS
**Health benefits of Nigerian traditional diets**
- URL: https://businessday.ng/health/article/health-benefits-of-nigerian-traditional-diets-1/
- KEY FINDINGS:
  - Hausa/Fulani: milk and grains dominant → different nutritional profile
  - Igbo: root vegetables, variety of soups, palm oil
  - Yoruba: starchy solids + vegetable soups/stews, very spicy
  - Amala (Yoruba yam flour staple): contains enzymes that help balance hormones in menopausal women
  - Yam: rich in vitamins B6, eases menopause symptoms, anti-inflammatory

### Quora / Legit.ng — NIGERIAN ETHNIC FOODS
- Yoruba: amala, gbegiri, ewedu, efo riro, ofada rice/ayamase, pounded yam
- Igbo: ofe nsala, ukazi soup, nkwobi, ofe onugbu (bitter leaf), egusi
- Hausa-Fulani: tuwo shinkafa, miyan kuka (baobab), suya, masa, kilishi, fura da nono
- Ijaw/Delta: banga soup, fresh seafood stews, starch

## NOTE ON RESEARCH GAPS (critical for Aunty Mel)

1. No published studies compare menopause at SUB-ETHNIC level within Africa
   (Yoruba vs Igbo vs Hausa, Kikuyu vs Luo, Zulu vs Xhosa)

2. UK-specific ethnic minority menopause data remains very limited
   (most data from US — may not reflect UK diaspora experience)

3. Diet-menopause interaction studies are almost entirely Western-centric

4. The "cultural" differences we can document most rigorously are:
   - Age of menopause onset (country level)
   - Symptom patterns (broad ethnic group level)
   - Dietary traditions and nutritional content (community/tribe level — well documented)
   - Cultural attitudes to menopause (community level — qualitative literature)

5. Pamela's clinical and community expertise fills the gap between research and lived experience.
   All cultural recommendations should be reviewed and approved by Pamela before deployment.

## CITATION FORMAT FOR IN-APP USE

When wellness recommendations reference research, use:
"Research shows..." (not "Studies prove..." — avoid overclaiming)
"Evidence suggests..." (for moderate-quality evidence)
"Traditionally used in [culture] for..." (for ethnobotanical/traditional practices)
