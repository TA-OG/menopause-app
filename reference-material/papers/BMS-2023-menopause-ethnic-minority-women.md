## Why is it important to improve inclusivity and address the differences in menopause experience in ethnic minority women?

The menopause transition is a major health milestone for women. Besides the symptoms that accompany menopause, other factors such as biological, psychological, behavioural and social changes shape a woman's midlife and future health.

The UK is a multi-ethnic, multi-racial country and, in 2018, 13.8% of the UK population had an ethnic minority background. There is however limited research evidence of menopause experiences in British ethnic minority women.

## Barriers to ethnic minority women seeking help

- Menopause is still a social stigma and a taboo subject in many minority communities
- Menopause may be considered as a symbol of loss of fertility and femininity
- There may be a general expectation for women to stay silent and not complain about their ailments
- A lack of knowledge about menopause and HRT
- Lack of menopause educational resources showing ethnic minority women
- Language barriers and healthcare professionals lacking understanding of terms used by ethnic minority women

## KEY FINDING: Sub-ethnic variations
"There can be significant sub-ethnic variations in symptoms within an ethnic group."
— BMS 2023, citing Im EO et al, J Transcult Nurs 2010

## Differences in menstrual patterns

- Chinese and Japanese women experience longer menstrual cycles than Caucasian women during menopause transition
- Women of African and Caribbean descent and Hispanic women have fewer ovulatory cycles in the last year before final menstrual period

## Differences in Oestradiol changes (SWAN study)

- Oestradiol begins to decrease ~2 years before final menstrual period
- Chinese and Japanese women have shorter span and slightly lower oestradiol levels than Caucasian women and women of African/Caribbean origin

## Distinctive characteristics — Women of African and Caribbean descent

- Mean age of natural menopause: 49.6 years (Palmer et al, Am J Public Health 2003)
- Longer duration of menopause transition
- Highest prevalence and longest duration of vasomotor symptoms — also more severe
- More likely to experience shorter sleep duration, more frequent awakenings, less efficient sleep
- Weight gain and mental health issues prominent
- Display SMALLER decline in sexual function — report greater importance of sex
- Even women with severe symptoms may find it difficult to come forward to seek help
- Higher allostatic load than Caucasian women (Richardson et al, SSM Popul Health 2021)

## Distinctive characteristics — South-East Asian women (China, Japan)

- May not complain of severe vasomotor symptoms
- Lower importance of sex; suffer more from low libido and sexual pain
- More forgetfulness, joint and muscle pains
- Lower bone mineral density but lower risk of osteoporotic fractures (explained by higher composite strength indices)

## Distinctive characteristics — South Asian women (India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka)

- Average age of menopause in Indian women (in India): 46.7 years (Kriplani & Banerjee, Maturitas 2005)
- Mean age of menopause in Pakistani women: 47.16 years (Qazi, J Coll Physicians Surg Pak 2006)
- Both significantly lower than Western countries (51 years)
- Premature Ovarian Insufficiency: prevalence 1.5% — women may not seek help due to completed family by mid-30s
- Implications for long-term health: CVD, dementia, osteoporosis
- Indian women complain more of vulval and uro-gynaecological symptoms
- Higher incidence of CVD risk factors: metabolic syndrome, insulin resistance, diabetes, central obesity, hypertension
- Discussions about sexual health challenging
- Mental health not discussed in communities
- Pakistani women: 78% unaware of menopausal symptoms and their effect on health (Nusrat et al, J Ayub Med Coll 2008)

## Self-help behaviours in ethnic minority women

- Taking natural supplements, herbal products, Chinese medicine
- Being mentally strong, focusing on relaxation
- Implementing lifestyle changes including exercise and diet
- Chinese women MORE open to seeing a doctor and taking medications than other ethnic minority women

## The concept of acculturation

"Attitudes of women towards menopausal symptoms may vary, depending on whether they are living in their country of origin, or if they have moved to a new country and environment. Attitudes and beliefs within a country, vary between traditional societies and more modern westernised societies."
— BMS 2023
