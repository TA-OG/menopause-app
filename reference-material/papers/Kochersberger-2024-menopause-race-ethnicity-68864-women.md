# Kochersberger A et al. (2024)
# The association of race, ethnicity, and socioeconomic status on the severity of menopause symptoms
# A study of 68,864 women
# Menopause. 2024 Jun 1;31(6):476-483
# DOI: 10.1097/GME.0000000000002349
# Source: Evernow telehealth platform, March 2019 – January 2023
# Downloaded summary: May 2026

---

## Study design
- Cross-sectional study, n=68,864 women
- Evernow online telehealth platform
- Modified Menopause Rating Scale
- Multivariate binomial generalised linear model
- Controlled for: race/ethnicity, age, BMI, smoking, bilateral oophorectomy, SES

## Demographic breakdown
- White: 77.4%
- Hispanic: 9%
- Black: 6.7%
- Two or more races/ethnicities: 4.4%
- Asian: 1.2%
- Indigenous: 0.8%
- Middle Eastern: 0.3%
- South Asian: 0.2%

## KEY FINDINGS — Odds Ratios vs White women

### Black women (higher severity):
- Hot flashes: OR 1.91 (97.5% CI 1.75–2.09, p<0.001)
- Night sweats: OR 1.87
- Sleep disturbances: OR 1.67
- Brain fog: OR 1.19
- Pain during intercourse: OR 1.38
- Unusual periods: OR 1.65

### Hispanic women (higher severity):
- Skin/hair changes: OR 1.58 (97.5% CI 1.45–1.71, p<0.001)

### Mixed race women:
- Skin/hair changes: OR 1.41 (97.5% CI 1.26–1.58, p<0.001)

### Indigenous women:
- Painful sex: OR 1.39 (97.5% CI 1.19–2.75, p=0.007)

### Middle Eastern women:
- Weight changes: OR 2.22 (97.5% CI 1.25–4.37, p=0.01)

### Asian and South Asian women:
- LOWER overall symptom severity vs white women

## AUTHORS' NOTE ON LIMITATIONS
- Sample skewed toward higher SES (online telehealth access)
- Underrepresentation of South Asian (0.2%) and Indigenous (0.8%) women
- Self-reported ethnicity
- No sub-ethnic breakdown (critical gap: no Yoruba vs Igbo, no Tamil vs Punjabi differentiation)
